?? Layer-by-Layer Trust Validation

?? Layer 1: CONSUMER IMPACT MET (Foundation)

Definition: Every piece of work in the OBINexus ecosystem must demonstrate measurable, positive impact on real end-users before it can support higher layers.

Validation Requirements:

  • Post-use consumer reporting with cryptographic timestamps
  • Demonstrated utility for actual users (not just developers)
  • Consumer feedback logged to audit channels
  • Problem-solution alignment verification

Guarantees: No theoretical solutions. No developer-only features. No code that doesn't solve real human problems.

Maintainers: All ecosystem participants validate this before any other work proceeds.

.rift.gov required AuraSeal binding Consumer testimony

?? Middle Leg: Contributor Engagement Protocol

Trust Flow: Contributors earn advancement through demonstrated consumer impact and technical excellence.

Progression Path:

  • Observer: Attends meetings, runs test modules, learns governance
  • Builder: Delivers working module with consumer utility proof
  • Steward: Maintains modules + mentors other contributors
  • Core: Consensus voting rights, ecosystem governance participation

Connects to Apex via: No contributor advancement without consumer impact validation at each level.

Milestone.json tracking Peer review gates Entropy checking

?? Middle Leg: Core Team Delivery

Trust Flow: Internal team members validate all external contributions and maintain system integrity.

Responsibilities:

  • Validators: Cryptographic verification of all commits and policy compliance
  • Maintainers: System reliability, security patches, infrastructure health
  • Researchers: Consumer need analysis, technical innovation, impact measurement

Connects to Apex via: Every core team deliverable must trace back to consumer impact evidence.

Git-RAF commits only Dual signature required Audit trail mandatory

?? Middle Leg: Customer Execution

Trust Flow: Paying customers ("solver-payers") can only claim delivery success when consumers validate impact.

Validation Process:

  • Customer defines problem with consumer context
  • Solution delivery must include consumer testing phase
  • Consumer sign-off required before payment release
  • Impact measurement continues post-delivery

Connects to Apex via: Customer satisfaction depends on consumer impact, creating incentive alignment.

Escrow protection Consumer veto power Impact metrics

?? Middle Leg: Milestone Governance Layer

Trust Flow: Funding, advancement, and project continuation all require consumer impact validation.

Governance Functions:

  • Audit: Continuous verification of consumer impact claims
  • Escrow: Funds held until consumer validation complete
  • Return Paths: Automatic rollback if consumer impact fails
  • Risk Flagging: Early warning system for consumer impact degradation

Connects to Apex via: No milestone advancement without cryptographically verified consumer impact.

Entropy-sealed validation Multi-sig requirements Automatic rollback

?? APEX: Aspirational Trust Infrastructure Met

Definition: A functioning ecosystem where innovation happens sustainably, with full traceability, zero ghosting, and consumer-first values embedded in every transaction.

Achieved When:

  • Zero-Ghosting: All communications tracked and acknowledged via No Ghosting Protocol
  • Cryptographic Governance: Every decision and change verified through .rift.gov and Git-RAF
  • Sustainable Innovation: New development driven by proven consumer need, not just technical possibility
  • Full Traceability: Every action traceable from consumer impact through governance to implementation

This level cannot exist unless ALL supporting pillars are live and cryptographically auditable.

Complete ecosystem operational Consumer sovereignty Governance automation

?? Critical Trust Chain Requirements

Foundation Rule: The triangle cannot stand without Layer 1 (Consumer Impact Met). Every higher layer depends on this base.

Validation Chain:

  1. Consumer impact must be cryptographically verified before any work advances
  2. Contributors cannot enter Core without consumer feedback on their work
  3. Customers cannot claim delivery success without consumer sign-off
  4. Investors cannot release next milestone funding without all pillar conditions greenlit
  5. Apex level only achieved when all layers operate autonomously with cryptographic validation

Enforcement Mechanism: Git-RAF policy validation, AuraSeal identity binding, and entropy checking ensure no layer can be bypassed or faked.
